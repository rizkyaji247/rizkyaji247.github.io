CASE-001

New Treasury Core System (NTCS)

Validated
Situation

NTCS is a new treasury platform (running on Front Arena) built to replace the bank's existing system, OPICS Summit, and planned to cover roughly 80% of Bank Mandiri's treasury transactions. It spans FX instruments (TOD, TOM, SPOT & derivatives), money market instruments (SBI, T-Bills, government securities/SUN and more), retail sukuk, Repo & Reverse Repo, top-up margin for Repo, and sharia deposit loans.

Task

I joined at Phase 2 to design test case scenarios from the FSD, TSD, and treasury user requirements. Core E2E SIT–UAT testing traced every transaction across five screens — Front Office (FO), Middle Office (MO), Back Office (BO), Reporting, and Accounting — with my scope covering FO, BO, Reporting, and Accounting, and raising defects in Jira.

Action

Wrote test scripts and executed scenarios, opening the Reporting screen in Front Arena (Operation & Trading Manager menu) to compare each field against the FSD and raising a Jira ticket on any mismatch. Cross-checked trade data between the front-office and Reporting screens, supported the accounting team in pulling the G/L journal from Front Arena for review, helped treasury users organize testing documentation, and authored a “how-to” execution guide as formal test documentation.

Result

Reached a 70% pass rate across 4,500+ executed test scenarios (within a 100,000+ scenario suite estimated from Phase 1) in SIT and UAT. Verified that a trade created in Front Office generates correctly, is re-checked against Back Office rules through to BO Confirmed status, populates every mapped field in Reporting, and reconciles against the accounting team's Excel journal. Surfaced defects including numerous field-copy mismatches against the FSD and trade-status bugs.

CASE-002

Trade Finance Migration — BMCI (Flexcube → Eximbills)

Validated
Situation

Regression testing and migration from the legacy system (Flexcube) to a new Trade Finance platform for the overseas branch (KLN) Bank Mandiri Cayman Island (BMCI), running on Eximbills Enterprise (EE) with Swift Alliance Access (SAA).

Task

Build the regression and migration test suite: execute 700+ UAT test cases (of a 4,000+ total) across Import LC, Export LC, and Bank-to-Bank products, using script templates adapted from another overseas branch (BMHK). Author new test scripts from user requirements and case recommendations from the APD team and internal Digital Wholesale Banking (DWB) officers, and support new users in adopting the trade finance system.

Action

Adapted the BMHK templates to BMCI's context and ran regression cycles covering field re-checks, end-to-end transactions (notably the BBFM product), and static-data validation. Beyond the reused E2E flow, I added an accounting-reconciliation step: after a trade was created and approved, I coordinated with the ASP group to extract the raw G/L data and passed it to the accounting team to confirm the journal matched the trade booked in the TF application. Coordinated deferred items into the Change Request (CR) circulation, and walked new users through the system.

Result

Passed 700+ test cases (field re-checks and E2E, especially BBFM), with 10 deferred into CR circulation (the application had no active developer support, as the vendor's contract had ended). Identified a cross-currency limitation the live BMHK deployment had never surfaced — the application was configured for USD only, so EUR trades failed at approval with a “currency not balanced” error, confirmed with APD as a requirement gap against BMCI's multi-currency need. Also detected a dormant-account anomaly during verification and traced it to the connected Service Operation Architecture (SOA) system, escalating it to the SOA owner rather than logging it against Trade Finance.

CASE-003

Credit Card Billing Fix — Core Banking

Validated
Situation

A billing issue surfaced in credit card processing on the core banking system: corporate credit card charges were incorrectly routed into individual customers' bills — a defect with direct customer and financial impact. A focused 3-month project was set up to resolve and verify the fix.

Task

Execute 85 test cases from the existing test script, and check and validate valid credit card numbers on IBM AS400, correctly distinguishing individual vs. corporate card accounts.

Action

Managed test-data provisioning as the main bottleneck: dummy card numbers arrived in bulk batches, but a large share were unusable for AS400 testing (expired or zero-balance), so I filtered and validated a usable set before each cycle. Ran the full 85-case suite on IBM AS400, verifying that corporate charges routed only to corporate bills and individual charges only to individual bills.

Result

All 85 test cases passed with no defects in scope, confirming the billing separation between individual and corporate credit cards was fixed before release. The project timeline was driven largely by test-data readiness rather than execution volume.

CASE-004

RM Tools — Relationship Manager Platform

In progress
Situation

A new application, developed from the ground up, to manage team groups and monitor transactions across funds, credit, third-party funds (DPK), treasury investment, Kopra, and supply & value chain management. It serves multiple employee segments and roles — Corporate Banking, Commercial Banking, Treasury Office, Sales Branch Management, and working teams — across modules including Portfolio, Customer Info, User Management, and Homepage. The full suite of 14,761 scenarios was shared across a QA team of roughly 40 testers, given the large number of role- and segment-based account combinations to verify.

Task

Design back-office test cases for employee account provisioning (segments and roles per company policy), and execute testing focused on the widget sections and the transaction data pulled from EDA and WDM, confirming API connectivity so the RMT web app renders without errors. Work spanned shakedown, smoke, UAT, regression, and migration phases.

Action

Wrote the back-office provisioning test cases, then executed 637 test scenarios focused on widget and data validation. For data checks, the EDA team supplied source data into a sheet, which I reconciled field-by-field against the RMT application to confirm consistency. Currently in an idle phase awaiting Phase 1 completion, I run informal daily smoke checks — regularly catching widgets that lose their API connection and error out in the UI — and report them.

Result

Executed 637 scenarios (of a 14,761 total suite), with 628+ passed and 73 defects surfaced — expected for a newly built application, and evidence of effective early-stage coverage.
